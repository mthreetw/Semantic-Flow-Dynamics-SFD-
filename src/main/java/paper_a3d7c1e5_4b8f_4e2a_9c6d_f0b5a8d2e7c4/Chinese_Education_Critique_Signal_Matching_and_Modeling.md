# A Critique of Chinese Education: Signal Matching and Modeling

---
## Abstract

Chinese education now faces a deep problem: what it excels at and what the era actually demands have severely diverged.

What it excels at is training students to produce standard answers on exams—what we call "signal matching." What modern society actually needs is the ability to build effective models and continuously adapt when facing new problems—what we call "modeling capability." These two things look similar but are fundamentally different.

Using a systems analysis toolkit, this paper attempts to explain: How did this misalignment form? Why was this system enormously successful in the past? Why is it now increasingly unsustainable? What forces prop it up, and what forces are slowly tearing it apart?

The paper analyzes the convergence of four historical forces (industrial standardization, Confucian culture, the imperial examination tradition, and resource constraints from prolonged poverty), reveals three positive feedback loops internal to the education system, and identifies nine erosion lines currently at work. It concludes that a fundamental contradiction has emerged between the capabilities the economy needs and the degree of control that power demands—and this is what makes reform hardest.

This paper offers no policy recommendations. It only attempts to explain why this system does what it does.

**Keywords:** Semantic Flow Dynamics, signal matching, modeling capability, positive feedback loop, Chinese education, critical thinking, logic

---

## I. The Problem

A structural gap exists between the education system's criterion (signal matching) and the capability that human civilization actually depends on (modeling capability). Using Chinese education as the modeling object, this paper applies the formal tools of Semantic Flow Dynamics to analyze the dynamics of this structural gap: what it is, by what mechanisms it is maintained, what is sacrificed and what is gained, who is sacrificed, and how the dynamics change when conditions change.

This paper provides no policy recommendations. It provides structural explanation—why this system does what it does, why it once succeeded, and why common reform paths are absorbed by the system's own dynamics.

## III. Learning, Education, and System

### 3.1 Learning

Framework definition: learning is change in xin. Semantic flow never stops; learning happens at every moment. No classroom, textbook, teacher, or exam is required. Learning is neutral—xin changing in any direction counts as learning.

### 3.2 Education

Education is not the same as learning. Education is directional learning—the direction is toward making people better. "Better" is a value choice; every educational thinker answers this question. Dewey said to provide experience that enables growth. Confucius said to teach according to individual capacity to cultivate gentlemen. Socrates said dialogue brings people closer to truth. The directions differ, but all presuppose a "better." In the framework's language: education provides conditions and direction for change in xin, with the direction pointing toward the expansion of capability and the enhancement of adaptability.

Education provides direction but cannot guarantee results—the Law of Black-box guarantees that transformation is unpredictable. Education can provide conditions; it cannot determine outcomes.

### 3.3 System

The education system replaces "better" with "more matching." The direction shifts from "making people better" to "making people's output match designated signals." This replacement is the distortion. The distortion is not from learning to system but from education to system—education itself has direction; the system swaps the direction.

The system's definition of "learning": producing collapse output that matches designated signals within a specified range.

### 3.4 Two Criteria

**Exam criterion:** The degree of match between collapse output and designated signals within a specified range. Closed matching.

**Modeling capability criterion:** The adaptability of collapse output when facing new reality. Open comparison. Science, economics, art, and engineering all rest on modeling capability.

Modeling capability needs a positive definition. In the framework's language: modeling capability is the ability to adjust direction based on the gap after semantic flow encounters reality. Signal matching measures the distance between output and a designated signal—whether the answer is correct. Modeling capability measures the distance between output and reality—whether the model's results match actual observations. The former is closed, with answers preset; the latter is open, with reality presetting no answers.

This distinction is not invented by this paper. The mental models theory from cognitive science (Johnson-Laird) holds that the essence of human reasoning is constructing and manipulating models in the mind, not matching answers stored in memory. Ericsson's deliberate practice research demonstrates that mere repetition does not produce expertise; adjustment targeting the gap does—repetition is the operation of signal matching; deliberate practice is the operation of modeling. The OECD and UNESCO list critical thinking as a core 21st-century competency; the research consensus is that critical thinking can be explicitly taught, but what it requires is not memorization—it is active analysis, questioning premises, and evaluating arguments. Different words, same thing: the source of capability lies not in signal matching but in transformation after semantic flow collides with reality.

The Law of Dissipation creates a structural blind spot. What a person has learned exists within their semantic flow—others cannot see it, and the person cannot fully express it either (the Law of Dissipation guarantees that encoding is necessarily lossy). But the system needs to determine "whether this person has learned," so it needs signal-end verification methods. Exams are not the only method—practical operations, experiments, internships, and portfolio reviews are all methods, but they differ in cost. Exams are the cheapest: they require no equipment, no physical site, no one-on-one interaction, and offer the highest degree of standardization. Poverty pushes the cheapest option into the default (Section IV expands on this). "Enforceable by power" is an additional layer, coming from power demand (S₂), not from the Law of Dissipation—what power needs is a controllable criterion, and exams happen to be controllable. Three forces—the unobservability created by the Law of Dissipation, the cost constraint created by poverty, and the controllability demand created by power—converge on the same solution: exams. What is explained here is "why exams" (the choice of verification method). Section IV explains "why signal matching" (the locking of the entire educational direction)—exams are one implementation of signal matching; the roots of signal matching go deeper than exams.

Modeling capability cannot be standardized—the Law of Black-box guarantees that the results of transformation are unpredictable, and unpredictable things cannot serve as criteria.

The gap between education and system is the object this paper models. The next section explains why this gap appeared and why it persists.

## IV. Initial Conditions: The Coupling of Four Forces

The structure of modern Chinese education is not a clean design. It is the product of four forces colliding.

### 4.1 Industrial Standardization

The Prussian model, imported in the late 19th and early 20th century. Its design goal: mass-produce standardized executors. Enclosed space, unified timetable, uniform signals, uniform criteria, mandatory attendance.

### 4.2 Confucian Culture

Respect for teachers, obedience to authority, moral cultivation. It provides native legitimacy for the school's power structure—"respect the teacher" is itself the justification.

### 4.3 Imperial Exam Residue

The imperial examination system operated for 1,300 years (Sui-Tang to 1905). Exams determine fate, memorization is the core operation, credentials lead to power—these were not invented by modern schools; they are products of the imperial exam loop that persisted through intergenerational transmission.

### 4.4 Poverty

The hardest force. China was extremely poor when it established its modern education system, and the disposable resources of most families remain limited to this day. Wealth disparity means the rich stopped playing this game long ago—study abroad, private tutors, international schools. Those who remain in the system have no alternative.

Memorization requires no laboratories, no equipment, no small classes, and no teachers with strong modeling capability—teachers only need to be able to memorize themselves. The rich never spend their days memorizing. Memorization is not the essence of education; it is the education of the poor—the lowest-cost solution under resource constraints.

### 4.5 Coupling

The four forces overlap at a single point—all demand signal matching. Industry demands execution to specification, Confucian culture demands conduct according to classics, the imperial exam tradition demands answers according to model essays, and poverty demands the cheapest operation. Signal matching becomes the intersection. The sacrifice is therefore quadrupled. The structure is especially stable because removing any one force leaves the other three still supporting it.

### 4.6 Probe: The Absence of Logic

Logic is not a subject; it is a filtering structure. Once students possess the filtering condition "Is this reasoning valid?"—this condition does not operate in just one course. The pedagogy of the Analerta shifts from memorization to argumentation, history courses require evidence chains, political education courses have their premises questioned. The exam criterion dissolves in the face of logical filtering. Insert logic as a probe into the system, and the system's rejection response is the diagnostic result.

Of the four forces, three reject, one does not reject, and one amplifies. Industry does not reject logic—engineers need logic, quality control needs logic, and Germany, where the Prussian model originated, has philosophy in high school. What rejects logic are the other three: the deep xin of Confucian culture ties the legitimacy of knowledge to authority rather than reasoning process; the imperial exam tradition's criterion tests matching rather than reasoning validity; political power (S₂) regards questioning of premises as a threat. Poverty does not directly reject logic, but under conditions where the other three already reject it, poverty provides an additional excuse—teaching logic costs more than teaching memorization.

Taiwan, Japan, and South Korea serve as control groups for this analysis. Japanese high schools have an "Ethics" course, but it teaches intellectual history, not reasoning training; in South Korea, philosophy's place in the curriculum is occupied by "Moral Education." The political conditions in these three places are completely different—Japan and South Korea have democratized, Taiwan has undergone political transformation—but logic is equally absent. Remove the political variable, and the rejection still occurs, proving that the Confucian culture force operates independently of politics. China's distinctiveness lies in politics and culture stacking in the same direction—a double lock.

The counterexample is France. Philosophy is a compulsory subject for all high school students, and the first exam of the baccalauréat is a four-hour philosophy essay. France does not teach philosophy as intellectual history but as concepts—freedom, rationality, morality—requiring students to deconstruct and construct arguments themselves. The root of this tradition is the Enlightenment: France's deep xin defines "questioning premises" as the foundation of civic literacy. Same probe, inserted into the French system, zero rejection. Different deep xin, different filtering structure, different system capacity.

The absence of logic is not the result of any single policy; it is the signature of the coupling structure. Analysis at the cognitive-structure level is an independent research direction, outside the scope of this paper.

## V. The Model

### 5.1 Cycle Structure

```
S₁ (economic demand: what capabilities are needed in people)
S₂ (power demand: how controllable people need to be)
  → Criterion (power sets the criterion: what to test. Responds to both S₁ and S₂)
    → Execution (schools execute: how to teach)
      → Output (output: what kind of people)
        → Reception (society receives: match or gap)
          → Feedback (feedback: can the pain reach the power end)
            → Criterion (power maintains criterion / changes anchor)

Output simultaneously flows back to Execution:
  Output → teachers (return to Execution as operators)
  Output → parents (send the next generation into Execution)
  Output → policymakers (enter Criterion, maintain the criterion)

Execution self-reinforcement:
  Output (admission rate) → Execution's authority → better students + more resources + local government support
  → Execution gains more power → Execution further reinforces the same operations → Output (higher admission rate)
```

Three closed loops. The main loop determines what the cycle runs. The self-replication loop determines who runs the cycle. The Execution self-reinforcement loop determines that the cycle runs ever harder. The three loops lock simultaneously and reinforce each other.

### 5.2 Definitions of Each Node

**S — Social demand.** S is not a single demand but the superposition of two. S₁ is economic demand: what capabilities are needed in people. S₂ is power demand: how controllable people need to be. Previously, S₁ and S₂ did not conflict—the economy needed standardized executors, power needed obedient people, same solution. Now S₁ and S₂ are torn apart—the economy needs modeling capability, but modeling capability means independent thinking, and independent thinking threatens S₂.

**Criterion — The anchor of the cycle.** All downstream nodes are held by the anchor. Current anchor: the gaokao, a signal matching test.

**Execution — School execution.** Current operation: memorization (the lowest-cost solution under the coupling of four forces). Execution self-reinforces through positive feedback—admission rate is authority capital, authority attracts students and resources, local governments use admission rates as political achievements. The Hengshui model is the product of Execution's self-reinforcement loop run to its extreme.

**Output — The cycle's output.** Output does not only enter Reception; it simultaneously flows back—becoming teachers, parents, policymakers. The cycle runs itself using its own products. Epistemic barrier (40a22ff8-9d90-4e1e-82f6-1fd80917c139.EpistemicBarrier) is transmitted intergenerationally here. Output is a model simplification: large numbers of people are screened out during Execution (dropouts, eliminations, middle school tracking). Those screened out are the cycle's fuel, not its products.

**Reception — Social reception.** Match (stability) or gap (reckoning).

**Feedback — Feedback.** The key variable: who feels the pain. Pain falls on power → the pathway opens. Pain falls on students → the pathway is severed.

### 5.3 Stable State

Positive feedback can run only if Output is actually useful. This premise held for a considerable time.

China used this cycle to pull an extremely poor country into industrialization at extremely low cost. The economy needed large numbers of people who could execute to specification (S₁), the cycle mass-produced such people (Output), the market received them (Reception), the payoff materialized (pass the exam → credential → job → income), the bottom saw returns and voluntarily sent the next generation into the cycle. Both S₁ and S₂ matched Output; the cycle ran stably. Forty years of economic growth did not happen outside the cycle—it was supported by the cycle's products.

Stability was triply reinforced: the self-replication loop (products flow back as operators), payoff-path positive feedback (returns visible, driving investment), and bottom-up voluntary support (the gaokao is the poor's only path upward—not coercion, but rational choice).

The problem with the cycle is not that it was always broken. It succeeded under the right conditions, and the conditions changed. The key is the distinction in Section 3.4: during the industrialization phase, what S₁ demanded was signal matching—execute to specification, produce to instruction, operate by procedure—and the cycle's output was exactly that. The gap between signal matching and modeling capability was not a problem in that phase because the market itself did not demand modeling capability. After S₁ shifted from "needing standardized executors" to "needing modeling capability," the cycle continued producing the same thing, but the market stopped accepting it. Section VI analyzes precisely this: after the turning point, how positive feedback shifts from self-reinforcement to self-erosion.

### 5.4 The Source of Modeling Capability Is Not in Schools

Modeling capability requires three conditions: encountering genuine problems with no preset answers, receiving feedback about the gap between output and reality, and having space to adjust the direction of semantic flow based on the gap. All three are indispensable. Exercises with preset answers are not genuine problems—the gap is eliminated. Right-wrong scoring is not gap feedback—it tells you how far you are from the designated signal, not how far from reality. Standard procedures give no adjustment space—the direction is locked.

Schools provide the exact opposite of all three conditions: preset answers, right-wrong scoring, standard procedures. Schools are not the source of modeling capability; they are a desert for it.

But school is not students' only signal environment. Family, peers, the internet, part-time work, romantic relationships—all are signal sources, all capable of providing genuine problems with no preset answers. Subject knowledge is material; modeling capability requires the personal experience of using that material to encounter reality. Chinese education does produce people with modeling capability—not because schools cultivate it, but because schools fail to occupy all available time.

### 5.5 Verification Through Extreme Closure: The Hengshui Model

If the source of modeling capability lies outside school, cutting off all external sources should lead to collapse.

China's super schools—Hengshui High School, Maotanchang High School—implement 24-hour militarized management. The results: adolescent depression screening rate of 24.6% (Chinese Academy of Sciences Institute of Psychology, 2021), psychiatric disorder rate of 17.5% (National Health Commission, 2021), non-suicidal self-injury rate of 27.4%. After suicides at Hengshui No. 2 High School, iron bars were installed on buildings. A wave of school withdrawals has spread to primary schools, with waiting lists for leave slots.

The primary force creating super schools is local governments' pursuit of admission-rate-based political achievements—directly verifying that schools answer only to those above. Installing iron bars is the physical manifestation of the cycle's self-protection: do not change the structure; block the exit of collapse.

### 5.6 Sacrifice Structure

Sacrificed: modeling capability, individual differences, the ability to question, space for xin's free change.
Gained: large-scale predictability, social operational efficiency, extremely low cost, a controllable population.
Who is sacrificed: those with no alternative. Those screened out sacrifice even more—they do not receive even the promised returns.

The trade is no longer worthwhile for S₁ (what is gained is no longer valuable), but it is still worthwhile for S₂ (what is sacrificed is exactly what power does not want).

## VI. The Instability of Positive Feedback

Positive feedback is not a perpetual motion machine. Nine erosion lines act simultaneously on every node of the cycle.

### Erosion Within Execution

**Student mental collapse.** Loop intensity exceeds the material's tolerance. The implosion of positive feedback itself.

**Midway attrition.** Those screened out sacrifice years and receive nothing; bottom-up support is weakened.

### Erosion of Execution's Fuel

**Population decline.** The student pool shrinks; Execution's self-reinforcement loop is starved of fuel.

**Emigration.** The best fuel flows out of the cycle.

### Erosion of the Payoff Path

**Credential inflation.** The premise "getting into college = a good job" is diluted.

**Economic slowdown.** The job market contracts; Reception-end absorptive capacity shrinks.

### Erosion of the Self-Replication Loop

**Lying flat.** Voluntary exit from the loop. No exams, no rat race, no children, no consumption. The supply line of self-replication is directly severed.

### Erosion of Closure

**Information penetration.** Students see post-graduation reality while still inside school. Closure is broken, accelerating lying flat and mental collapse.

### Erosion of S-End and Cost-End

**AI.** Simultaneously depresses the market value of signal matching, provides cheaper personalized education alternatives, and pulls out the underlying premise that makes credentials valuable. The most powerful line, but not the only one.

Nine lines act simultaneously; every one of the three loops is leaking.

## VII. Empirical Verification

The model's predictions can be checked line by line against currently observable phenomena.

### 7.1 Payoff Path Collapse

Zhaopin Recruitment's 2024 report shows a fresh graduate employment rate of only 55.5%. The 2025 graduating class nationwide is projected at 12.22 million (Ministry of Education, 2024). National Bureau of Statistics data show that the surveyed urban unemployment rate for 16-to-24-year-olds (excluding students) peaked at 18.9% (August 2025). Average starting salary for bachelor's degree holders is only 4,526 yuan, rising just 800 yuan over a decade—less than skilled workers and food delivery riders (Zhaopin, 2026).

Postgraduate exam registrations fell from 4.38 million to 3.43 million, declining for three consecutive years. Civil service exam registrations broke through 3.51 million, surpassing postgraduate exam numbers. Young people are shifting from pursuing credentials to seeking stability—the payoff from credentials is collapsing.

### 7.2 Lying Flat and Voluntary Exit

Ministry of Civil Affairs statistics show that marriage registrations in 2024 fell to 6.106 million couples, down 20.5% from 2023, the lowest since 1980 (Ministry of Civil Affairs, 2025). The term "rat people" has garnered billions of views on social media. Young people state plainly: "I can't even support myself"—with no plans for marriage or children.

### 7.3 Emigration

Young people state openly: "Walk out onto the street, and whole stretches of shops are shuttered"—and ultimately choose to leave China.

### 7.4 Information Penetration and the "Reading Is Useless" Discourse

"The old logic of learning is failing; many families feel that past modes of investment and assumptions about good education are gradually becoming ineffective." The discourse of "reading is useless" triggers anxiety in some populations—the gap between imagination and reality has struck the traditional belief that "study leads to success."

### 7.5 AI Erosion

Multiple recruitment platforms report that large and medium enterprises are deploying AI resume screening systems at scale, with the weight of credentials in initial screening declining continuously.

### 7.6 Paths That Bypass the School

Douyin, livestreaming, self-media—the capabilities demanded are all modeling capabilities. No credentials needed; direct connection to market. The school cycle's moat is being bypassed.

### 7.7 The Tutoring Industry

When parents have money, that money is fed into the same cycle—cram schools, workbooks, repetitive drills. This verifies the intergenerational transmission of epistemic barrier: when the cycle's products become consumers, they purchase more of the cycle.

### 7.8 Comparison

Every one of the model's nine erosion lines matches the real-world data. The gap is not a prediction; it is a fact already happening, already large enough to be directly visible in statistics and to form social consensus.

The model's falsification conditions are equally explicit: the reversal of any erosion line's direction—youth unemployment declining persistently while credential premium recovers, marriage and birth rates rebounding, the emigration wave reversing, lying flat receding with a rising proportion of active participation in the loop—would constitute partial falsification of the model. If most erosion lines reverse simultaneously, the model's claim of "simultaneous erosion" would require fundamental revision. The model is not post-hoc explanation; it is a structural prediction that can be overturned by future data.

## VIII. Power's Response

### 8.1 Power Is Already Moving

In 2026, the Ministry of Education and four other departments issued the "AI + Education" Action Plan. Power's judgment, translated into the framework's language: stop cultivating "finished products"; cultivate people who can rapidly build cognitive frameworks in unknown domains, integrate resources across disciplinary boundaries, and iterate continuously in the face of failure. This is modeling capability. Power sees the gap, and the judgment is correct.

### 8.2 But the Mode of Execution Verifies Cycle Self-Protection

Power's approach is not to dismantle the structure but to stuff AI into the old structure. AI is used for intelligent grading, Q&A, learning analytics. Artificial intelligence becomes a new course, a new exam subject. The gaokao remains; unified textbooks remain; standardized assessment remains.

The same dynamics as "quality education becoming a new exam subject." The cycle captures the institutionalizable facets and filters out the non-institutionalizable ones.

### 8.3 The Criterion Has Not Changed; the Structure Has Not Changed

As long as the gaokao remains a signal matching criterion, all teaching activities are ultimately pulled back to signal matching. Criterion is the cycle's anchor; without changing the anchor, the ship cannot move.

The Ministry of Education is reducing enrollment or suspending majors with low employment rates and increasing enrollment in AI and integrated circuits—but the mode of operation remains adjusting what to test and what to recruit, not changing the anchor. Criterion has not changed.

## IX. The Triple Resistance to Changing the Anchor

### 9.1 The Feedback Pathway Is Severed

The pain falls on students, not on power. The cumulative effect of the nine lines is changing the conditions of Feedback—mass unemployment threatens stability, mental collapse becomes a social issue, lying flat and not reproducing weaken national strength—but the transmission path is long and indirect.

### 9.2 The Collapse Cost of Changing the Anchor

All interest structures attached to the old anchor collapse simultaneously. This is a bone marrow transplant—first kill the old; there is a gap period in between; and the donor does not exist. Modeling capability cannot be standardized for assessment (a hard constraint from the Law of Black-box); what anchor to change to, no one in the world has an answer. Finland is groping; Singapore is groping; no one has figured it out.

Historical parallel: the abolition of the imperial examination in 1905. People who had spent their lives studying the Four Books and Five Classics became worthless overnight. The scale of a contemporary anchor change is orders of magnitude larger than the imperial examination.

### 9.3 The Contradiction Between S₁ and S₂

The deepest resistance. Changing the anchor satisfies S₁ (the economy needs modeling capability) but threatens S₂ (power needs controllable people). Independent thinking, the ability to question—the economy needs them, the regime fears them. No solution simultaneously satisfies both.

Power does not change the anchor not because it fails to see the problem, not merely because the change cost is high, but because it fundamentally does not want the result of changing the anchor.

## X. Conclusion

### 10.1 Three Paths

**No anchor change.** The nine lines continue to erode, the economic end continues to deteriorate, but S₂ is protected. Chronic death.

**Anchor change.** The collapse cost is enormous, the donor does not exist, and even success threatens S₂. A bone marrow transplant with an unknown donor, where a successful transplant may trigger the immune system to attack the host.

**Stuff AI into the old structure.** The current choice. Take medicine to stay alive, buy time. It does not resolve the contradiction but does not trigger collapse. Performing a bone marrow transplant without a viable donor is suicide—this is not stupidity; it is rationality.

### 10.2 The Certain and the Uncertain

Economic-end collapse is structural and unavoidable. The gap between S₁ and Output will only widen.

Political-end collapse is not certain. Power can choose to absorb the economic cost to maintain S₂. Throughout history, numerous regimes have persisted for a long time under terrible economic conditions.

The education system is trapped in the contradiction between S₁ and S₂, with no solution that simultaneously satisfies both. This is not a problem that can be optimized; it is a structural contradiction.

### 10.3 Fractal

This paper models a single cross-section: education. But the model's structure does not depend on education's specificity. The contradiction between S₁ and S₂, the three loops of positive feedback, the nine erosion lines, the triple resistance to anchor change—these are not unique to education. The same people, carrying similar xin structures, enter economics, law, politics, and media, producing similar structures in every domain. Education is one cross-section of this structure, not the pathology. The same pattern self-replicates at different scales—this is a fractal. East Asian societies share Confucian culture and exam-selection traditions; the model's variables are the intensity of S₂ and the proportional mix of the four forces; the structure remains the same. Further refinement is future work.

### 10.4 Reuse

This paper's model can be aimed at any system composed of people, possessing a power structure, and running positive feedback loops. Each of the nine erosion lines can be independently expanded into a complete study. Cross-national comparison, micro-level verification (field observation of a single classroom), and cross-domain reuse (analysis of the S₁ and S₂ contradiction in economics, judiciary, and media) are all follow-up work unfolding from the same model. This paper is a node, not an endpoint.

### 10.5 Boundary

The framework does not predict paths or timing. The framework delivers its conclusions here. What comes after is people's business.

This paper's expression is a signal. Readers' xin differs, and the semantic flow that emerges from reading differs. This paper cannot exempt itself from its own postulates.
